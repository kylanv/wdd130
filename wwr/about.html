History

White water rafting in America traces its roots to indigenous river navigation, evolving into a recreational pursuit in the mid-19th century with expeditions down rivers like the Colorado. The sport gained popularity in the mid-20th century with the rise of outdoor enthusiasts, but it wasn't until the 1970s and 1980s that commercial rafting truly took off, with outfitters offering guided trips on rivers nationwide, cementing its status as a thrilling adventure activity enjoyed by many.
